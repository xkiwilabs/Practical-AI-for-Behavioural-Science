The Viva Exam

Week 12 — What to Expect & How to Prepare

Your oral exam, demystified

The Viva at a Glance

  • 30% of your final grade
  • 15 minutes, one-on-one, in person
  • Covers Weeks 1–11
  • Three sections: Definitions · Short Answer · Study Pitch

It's a conversation, not a trap. We want to see that you understand the ideas and can talk through them. Reasonable answers in your own words are exactly what we're after.

Section 1 Concept Definitions

  • 10 concepts, drawn at random from a pool of 40
  • About 20 seconds each (~4 minutes total)
  • Max 4 points each — 40 total
  • Graded on technical accuracy (75%) + conciseness (25%)

One clear sentence beats a rambling paragraph. You have the full list of 40 terms — practise saying each definition out loud.

Section 2 Short Answer

  • 3 research scenarios — which ML/AI method fits, and how would you apply it?
  • 30 seconds to plan, then up to 1 minute to answer (you'll see the question first)
  • Max 12 points each — 36 total
  • Graded on clarity (40%) + appropriateness & accuracy (60%)

This tests general understanding — not PhD-level depth. Pick a sensible approach, say what you'd do and what you'd watch out for. A clear one-minute answer that hits the main ideas is plenty.

9 example questions are released for practice. Your exam questions come from a separate set, but they test the same kinds of reasoning.

Section 3 Study Proposal Pitch

  • A 2-minute elevator pitch for a study that uses a method from the course
  • Then 30 seconds on how you used an LLM to develop it
  • 24 points: clarity (30%) + accuracy (30%) + innovation (40%)
  • Prepare in advance — but no notes or slides on the day

Pick a question you genuinely find interesting, match the method to it, and show how you used (and checked) an LLM to shape the idea.

How to Prepare

Your study materials

  • concept-list.md — the 40 terms + a glossary of supporting concepts
  • short-answer-examples.md — 9 worked examples with model answers
  • viva-terms.csv & viva-questions.csv
  • assessments/viva-exam.md — the full rubric

What works

  • Practise out loud and time yourself
  • Aim to understand, not memorise word-for-word
  • Use an LLM as a study partner — then verify what it tells you

The Practice App

How to run it

  1. Open the practice-app folder
  2. Double-click index.html — it opens in your browser
  3. Click Start Practice and go!

No install, no terminal, works offline.

What it does

  • A full timed run-through of all three sections
  • Fresh random terms & questions each run
  • Optional: record your voice and play it back to check timing & clarity

If you record, the app shows a live level meter so you can see your mic is working — the audio stays on your device and is never uploaded. (Mic not moving? Check your browser's microphone permission and that the right input device is selected.)

On the Day

  • You'll have an individual time slot — check your schedule
  • No notes, slides, or aids during the exam
  • Take a breath, think for a moment, then answer

You've done the work — show us how you think. Good luck!
