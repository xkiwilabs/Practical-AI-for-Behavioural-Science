Best Model

Random Forest achieved the highest AUC (0.920) with accuracy 81.5% and F1 0.821. Logistic Regression was nearly identical (AUC 0.911). Decision Tree lagged behind (AUC 0.871). All three crushed the 54.5% baseline.

Key Predictors

PANAS negative affect (0.140) and GAD-7 anxiety (0.127) dominated. Then stress coping (0.080), sadness (0.073), and isolation (0.072). Personality and demographics contributed less — the daily mood and anxiety measures captured most of the signal.

Threshold Justification

For a screening tool, threshold 0.3 is better: recall jumps from 0.77 to 0.99 (catches nearly everyone), though precision drops to 0.77. For depression screening, missing a case is worse than a false alarm — you can follow up, but you can't help someone you didn't identify.

Ethical Consideration

The model was trained on COVID-era data from a predominantly female, US sample. It may not generalise to other populations or times. Using it to deny services (e.g., “you scored low, so no counselling”) would be harmful — models should flag people for support, never gate-keep access.

Refactoring Win

Created an evaluate_model() function that takes any fitted classifier and returns a metrics dictionary. Replaced ~40 lines of repeated code with 4 one-line calls. Made it trivial to add new models or change metrics.
