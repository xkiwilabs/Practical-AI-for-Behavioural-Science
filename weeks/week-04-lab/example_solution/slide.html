Best Model

All three regression models performed nearly identically: CV MAE ≈ 8.19, CV R² ≈ 0.339. With 38K rows and 11 features, regularisation barely matters — the data is too "wide" for overfitting. Linear regression is the winner for its interpretability.

Feature Importance

Emotional Stability dominates (coef ≈ −3.4) — a 1-point increase predicts a 3.4-point drop in depression. Then Extraversion (−1.4), Conscientiousness (−1.1), education (−0.6), Openness (−0.5), and Agreeableness (−0.5). Demographics add very little beyond personality.

Psychological Interpretation

Emotional Stability (inverse of neuroticism) being the strongest predictor is consistent with decades of personality–psychopathology research. The Big Five explain ~34% of depression variance — strong for individual-level prediction from brief personality measures.

Overfitting Check

Training R² = 0.339, CV R² = 0.339, Test R² = 0.334. Virtually no gap — no overfitting. The learning curves confirm this: training and validation errors converge early.

Debugging Story

First attempt to load the CSV failed silently — got 39,775 rows × 1 column. The file was tab-separated, not comma-separated. Fix: adding sep="\t" to pd.read_csv(). Lesson: always check df.shape and df.head() right after loading.
